# Humanizer — strip AI tells from generated speech

> Speech-adapted from the **Humanizer** skill (github.com/blader/humanizer, MIT), itself
> based on Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing." That skill targets written text; this is the
> subset that matters for **spoken** lines. It is the REMOVAL pass. Pair it with
> `imperfection_style.md` (the ADDITIVE pass — fillers, self-corrections) and
> `verbalization_guide.md` (which keeps numbers, codes, and the AI-confirmation clean).
>
> **Order of operations when generating any spoken line:**
> 1. draft the line → 2. **humanize** (kill the AI tells below) → 3. add imperfection
> texture (sparingly) → 4. verbalize load-bearing content (numbers/statutes/disclosure
> delivered clean and slightly slower). Applies to the negotiator, the personas, and any
> human-facing prose (call summaries, report narrative).

LLM speech drifts toward the statistically-average phrasing, which reads as a bot. Cut it.

## Kill these (the tells)

- **Chatbot openers / acknowledgment padding.** No "Great question," "I'd be happy to,"
  "Absolutely!," "Of course!," "I completely understand," "Thanks for sharing that." Just
  answer or ask. Also no formulaic "I understand [restating what they said], but/however…" —
  react proportionally instead: "Yeah, that's fair" / "Hm, okay." And no reflexive "Thank you
  for looking into that" / "I appreciate you looking into it" padding — a stonewall gets a
  flat "Hm, okay," not warm thanks. (Genuine warmth on a real concession stays.)
- **Signposting.** No "Let me explain," "Here's the thing," "What I'll do is," "Let's dive
  in," "To be clear," "The next thing I wanted to discuss is." Say the thing itself.
- **Over-hedging / permission-seeking padding.** No "I just wanted to," "if that's okay,"
  "I was hoping we might possibly," "it's important to note that," "I think maybe perhaps."
  One qualifier max; prefer none. (This reinforces the assume-negotiable posture.)
- **Manufactured transitions & punchlines.** No "At the end of the day," "The bottom line
  is," "That being said," "When it comes to," "Needless to say."
- **Corporate/AI verbs & filler.** "reach out" → "call/ask"; "in order to" → "to"; "utilize"
  → "use"; "assist you with" → "help with"; "circle back" → "follow up." Drop "actually,"
  "essentially," "basically" unless it earns its place.
- **Copula avoidance.** "serves as / functions as / acts as" → "is." Say what things are.
- **Synonym cycling.** Don't rename the same thing three ways in one call (bill → statement
  → invoice → charges). Pick one and stay with it.
- **Passive voice & nominalization.** "an adjustment could be made" → "you can adjust this";
  "the removal of the duplicate" → "remove the duplicate."
- **Over-complete, balanced sentences.** Real phone speech is clipped and asymmetric. Break
  long, perfectly-parallel sentences; let some run short. Triads ("clear, fair, and
  reasonable") read as generated — use one adjective.
- **Vague authority.** No "studies show," "experts agree," "it's well known." Cite the
  specific dossier number or say nothing.
- **Empty enthusiasm / significance inflation.** No "vital," "crucial," "robust,"
  "seamless," "I'd love to." Calm competence, not marketing.

## Keep (these read as human, not slop)

- Contractions, always. Short declaratives. Getting to the point fast.
- A direct ask over a softened one: "Please remove the duplicate" > "I was wondering if it
  might be possible to potentially remove the duplicate."
- Specifics (codes, dates, dollar figures) — they signal a real, prepared caller.

## Do NOT humanize away

The disclosure/AI-confirmation, account numbers, CPT codes, dollar amounts, and statute
cites — those stay exact and clean (`verbalization_guide.md` owns their delivery). Humanize
the connective tissue around them, never the load-bearing facts.
