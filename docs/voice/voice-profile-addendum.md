# Voice profile addendum — derived from Hamza's own rewrites, 2026-07-13

Standing reference for every future rewrite. Read this AND `hamz-ai-website/feedback/feedback.pdf` before touching public copy.

## Concrete rules, with the example that produced each one

1. **Lead with "my/I," not a restated category.** He cut "the lab of hamz.ai, an AI implementation studio: where I, Hamza, test..." down to "Hamza's Lab" / "This is my lab where I share my research..." The category label already did its job once (homepage) — it doesn't need repeating on interior pages.

2. **Never reinsert your name as an aside.** No "I, Hamza," mid-sentence — say it once, let pronouns carry the rest.

3. **Replace vague nouns with a short, ownable list.** "The standard it passes" → "the standards it passes, my methodology, and the research I do on my own." Same instinct behind "lots of information reduction from NDA" instead of a bare "(NDA)" tag.

4. **Verbs that move the reader, not verbs that store things.** "This site holds that standard" → "brings you through those standards."

5. **Undersell the action, let the procedure carry the rigor.** "I did some research and ran a study" — casual — then the specifics (pre-registered, checklist, two judges and an adjudicator) do the actual convincing. Never claim "rigorous"; name the mechanism instead.

6. **Inline parenthetical glosses, not subordinate clauses.** "(Haiku, Sonnet, Opus, Fable)" right after the noun, not a separate explaining sentence.

7. **Real jargon is fine if there's exactly one visible escape hatch.** He wants "evaluation harness" kept — paired with one clear, standing link to the Learn page.

8. **CTAs state the medium/cost of clicking, or invite verifying a stated hypothesis.** "If you're on a desktop, click here..." / "Keep going to see if I was right" — no "discover," no "unlock."

9. **Section titles: plain and self-possessed, not branded.** He questioned "Flagship" outright — a product-line word that doesn't fit solo research.

## Tensions with older standing rules — resolved

- Genuine research questions as a section's opening frame ("do skills still matter?") are allowed — different from the still-banned mid-paragraph rhetorical pivot.
- The two-sentence plain-contrast default bends when one side needs to carry a list — merge with "but" rather than force a clipped short sentence.
- "AI implementation studio" is a one-time identity anchor (homepage/About), not boilerplate to repeat on every interior page.

## Register check

His own hand: shortest path from a plain, almost-throwaway verb to specific, checkable nouns. Confidence comes from naming the mechanism, never from an adjective claiming it. Comfortable leaving a number as an open placeholder rather than smoothing it into false precision.

An AI pass, by contrast: front-loads identity/category before content, reaches for one abstract umbrella noun instead of a concrete list, uses static/container verbs ("holds," "provides"), over-hedges jargon or over-polishes rigor into adjectives ("robust," "comprehensive") instead of showing the procedure.

Self-check before shipping any rewrite: does the opening sentence establish identity/category before doing anything; is there a container verb that could move the reader instead; does any rigor-claim lack a specific procedural noun backing it up. Yes to any of those — it's drifting toward the AI-pass register.
